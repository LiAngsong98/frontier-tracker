## Example specialized profile: ecosystem diversity

```text
Profile name: county-ecosystem-diversity
Core topics: ecosystem diversity, ecosystem type diversity, ecosystem classification, ecosystem typology, ecosystem mapping
Secondary topics: spatial expression, county-scale assessment, remote sensing support, GIS-based ecosystem analysis
Study systems or organisms: ecosystem units rather than species or populations
Ecosystems or regions: county scale, regional landscapes, terrestrial and mixed socio-ecological county contexts
Methods or data types: ecosystem classification, typology construction, GIS, remote sensing, spatial metrics, index design
Pollutants, stressors, or focal variables: not fixed; prioritize diversity structure, ecosystem-unit difference, and spatial organization
Exclusion topics: biomedical ecosystem metaphors, business ecosystem metaphors, pure land-use pattern papers with no ecosystem diversity framework
Must-track journals: journals that directly publish ecosystem classification, ecology theory, and spatial ecology studies
Nice-to-track journals: adjacent geography, remote sensing, and conservation journals
Weekly reading capacity: 6 papers
Reminder threshold in days: 10
Preferred summary language: Chinese
Zotero collection or library target:
Obsidian vault path:
Obsidian folder:
High-relevance cues: explicit ecosystem diversity target, defensible ecosystem units, clear distinction from landscape or land-use diversity, county-scale applicability
Medium-relevance cues: reusable methods, proxy-path evaluations, typology or mapping frameworks that can be adapted
Low-relevance cues: broad biodiversity discussion without ecosystem-unit analysis
Boundary concepts to track separately: landscape diversity, land-use diversity, habitat heterogeneity, spectral diversity
Concept confusion red flags: land-use types treated as ecosystem types, landscape diversity treated as ecosystem diversity, proxy indicators presented as direct measures
```
