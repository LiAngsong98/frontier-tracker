# Example Research Profile

This is a generic example to help you create your own profile. Copy it, rename it, and fill in your specific research interests.

## Profile

- **Profile name:** my-research-topic
- **Core topics:** (your main research keywords — papers matching these are highest priority)
- **Secondary topics:** (adjacent themes that are useful but not your primary focus)
- **Study systems or organisms:** (what systems do you study? e.g., forests, wetlands, urban areas, coral reefs)
- **Ecosystems or regions:** (geographic focus, e.g., temperate forests, tropical regions, specific countries)
- **Methods or data types:** (what methods matter to you? e.g., remote sensing, field experiments, meta-analysis, GIS)
- **Exclusion topics:** (what should be filtered out? e.g., purely clinical studies, business/management papers)
- **Must-track journals:** (journals you always want to see)
- **Nice-to-track journals:** (journals you want to see when papers are relevant)
- **Weekly reading capacity:** (how many papers can you realistically read per week?)
- **Reminder threshold in days:** (after how many days should unread high-priority papers be flagged?)
- **Preferred summary language:** (English, Chinese, etc.)

## Keyword tiers

These keywords drive automatic paper screening. The screen_by_profile.py script uses them to classify papers into tiers.

### Core keywords

Papers matching these are classified as **core** — directly on-topic.

```text
keyword-1
keyword-2
keyword-3
```

### Proxy keywords

Papers matching these are classified as **proxy** — adjacent methods or themes.

```text
proxy-keyword-1
proxy-keyword-2
```

### Eco-context keywords

Papers matching these are classified as **eco** — broader ecological context.

```text
eco-keyword-1
eco-keyword-2
```

## Example: Wetland Carbon Flux

```text
Profile name: wetland-carbon-flux
Core topics: wetland carbon cycling, methane flux, restoration ecology
Secondary topics: microbial mediation, remote sensing support data
Study systems or organisms: coastal wetlands, estuaries, marsh plants
Ecosystems or regions: East and Southeast Asia
Methods or data types: chamber flux measurement, eddy covariance, stable isotopes, mixed models
Exclusion topics: pure marine fisheries, urban planning without ecosystem process data
Must-track journals: Global Change Biology, Environmental Science and Technology
Nice-to-track journals: Journal of Applied Ecology
Weekly reading capacity: 6 papers
Reminder threshold in days: 10
Preferred summary language: English
```

## Example: Urban Ecology

```text
Profile name: urban-ecology
Core topics: urban biodiversity, urban ecosystem services, green infrastructure, nature-based solutions
Secondary topics: urban heat island, urban planning, landscape connectivity
Study systems or organisms: urban parks, green roofs, street trees, urban waterways
Ecosystems or regions: global urban areas, with focus on rapidly urbanizing regions
Methods or data types: remote sensing, field surveys, spatial analysis, social-ecological modeling
Exclusion topics: purely architectural design, non-ecological urban studies
Must-track journals: Nature Cities, Landscape and Urban Planning, Urban Forestry & Urban Greening
Nice-to-track journals: Ecological Applications, Conservation Biology
Weekly reading capacity: 5 papers
Reminder threshold in days: 7
Preferred summary language: English
```
